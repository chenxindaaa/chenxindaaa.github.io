Effective Academic Writing

1 Effective Academic Writing

It's not easy to understand new complex scientific information unless it's being explained clearly.

1.1 Logical flow and structure

1.1.1 Be concise: Do not overwhelm your readers with new information

  • Sentence length: most of the sentences 10 to 20 words. Sometimes use a short sentence 5 or 6 to emphasize a key point. Sometimes have a sentence that's 30 or 35 words to thoroughly communicate a complex idea.
  • One idea at a time: present one idea before introducing the next one.
  • Avoid unnecessary words: Can you delete the word(s) without changing the meaning of the sentence?

1.1.2 Be specific: Ensure your words accurately reflect your ideas

  • Be careful with pronouns: This, that, these, those, they, it
  • Avoid qualitative words to describe quantitative data: Some, most, about, few
  • Avoid subjective words: Surprisingy, strikingly, interestingly These words can be used sparingly to highlight the most important findings in your study.

1.1.3 Be logical: Guide your readers from one to the next

  • Use sentence structure to guide your readers to the correct interpretation. Logically structing your sentences:
  • [ ] You deserve a raise, but the budget is tight.

  • [x] The budget is tight, but you deserve a raise (Stress position).

  • Use sentence structure to guide your reader to the next idea Signposting(linking) words: The budget is tight, but you deserve a raise. Your salary (topic position) will increase at the beginning of next year. > The topic position introduces the idea of the current sentence. ## 1. 2 Improving readability ### 1.2.1 Active voice (75%active 25%passive) > Sentences written in the active voice are more simple, direct, and easier to read.

Writing style guides encourage active voice: When use passive voice? - Avoid repeatition - Unclear subject: when you don't know who or what the subject is, that's probably a good time to acually use passive voice. - [ ] High temperature increases fungal containation of wheat. - [x] Fungal containation of wheat was increased during perods of high temperature. - Method section - [ ] We perform this technique under these conditions... - [x] The technique was performed under these conditions... - Sentence structure

1.2.2 Strong verbs

Avoid nominalizations (converting a verb into a noun). - [x] Estimate - [ ] Estimation - [x] Decide - [ ] Decision - [x] Confirm - [ ] Confirmation - [x] Correlate - [ ] Correlation

1.2.3 Keeping simple

Avoid complex words. Prepare a list of frequently used words to describe different actions. ## 1.3 Efficient writing strategies ### 1.3.1 Organize your ideas Write outline ### 1.3.2 Comminicate your ideas Write manuscript. Focus on clearly communicating those ideas.
